Time to get our hands dirty. Before we start covering SOME of the many different types of beer that are available we need to cover some keyword. First, ABV which stands for Alcohol by volume. This number is a percent that ranges from 2% to 12%. The higher the percent the more alcohol is in it. The other term worth knowing and you will see at breweries is IBU which stands for International Bitterness Units Scale. This number tells you the preceived bitterness scale. This is provided by the hops used during brewing. Once again the higher the number the more bitter of a taste you will find in your beer.


  • Pilsner is a beer usually straw or golden in color. Pilsners are a great beer to start at. This is because they are similar to what most people have had in Budweiser and Miller products. Pairs well with lighter foods: chicken, salads, salmon and bratwurst.

  • Blonde is a beer that is pale in color like a pilsner. They have a nice crisp dry taste to them. A slightly sweet beer with very little bitterness. Pairs well with lighter foods chicken, salads, salmon, bratwurst, and is great with pizza.

  • Wheat is a beer that is brewed with a lot of wheat along with malted barley. This gives wheat beer a distinct cloudy appearance. They taste a little sharper and have hints of banana and cloves. One of the most popular wheat beers in Michigan is Bells Oberon, which usually is timed with the arrival of summer in Michigan. Pairs well with medium to spicy foods. A lighter wheat beer will also go well with grilled fish.

  • Ales is a type of beer brewed from malted barley using warm-fermenation. Ales taste sweet, often have a full bodied fruity taste. Pairs well with lighter foods, salads and seafood, and with lighter desserts.

  • Amber Ales is a type of ale that is also brewed with a crystal malt to produce a nice rich amber color to the beer. Pairs well with roast beef, burgers, pizza, fried meats, cold sandwiches, and aged sharp cheeses.

  • Pale Ale is a beer made with warm fermentation with pale malts. The pale malts give it a bitter flavor and a light brown color. Pairs well with cheese, pasta, and BBQ.

  • American IPA is a newer style of pale ale that is made with large quanities of American hops. Its development began in the early 1980's. Pairs well with strong, spicy foods and bold flavored appetizers.

  • India Pale Ale Is a beer that was first brewed back in England around the 19th century. A light brown color with a bitter peppery taste. Pairs well with grilled seafood, shellfish, Indian Cuisine, Cajun, Creole, and aged bleu cheeses.

  • Porter A dark colored beer. A hoppy beer made from brown malt. Pairs well with red meats, bold cheeses, and shellfish.

  • Stout is a dark colored beer made with hops, water, yeast, and either roasted malt or roasted barley. Pairs well with red meats, bold cheeses, and shellfish.
